Standalone Robotics: Sound-based Robot Collaboration System with On-Device Foundation Model

Semin Ahn1, Hwancheol Kang1, Stefan Kind1,2, Hyeongwon Kim1, Sung-Hoon Ahn1,3*

1 Department of Mechanical Engineering, Seoul National University, Seoul, Republic of Korea
2 Department of Mechanical Engineering, RWTH Aachen University, Aachen, Germany
3 Institute of Advanced Machines and Design, Seoul National University, Seoul, Republic of Korea

* Corresponding author. Email: ahnsh@snu.ac.kr

Paper Code Appendix Video

We present Standalone Robotics, a sound-based, network-free multi-robot collaboration system that runs entirely on-device. Our framework fuses a compact 3-Dimensional Acoustic Ranging (3DAR) sensor for robust sound-source localization with an inaudible MFSK communication protocol (17–22 kHz) and an on-device multi-modal foundation model (MMFM) operating in a Plan–Observe–Act loop. Deployed on humanoid and quadruped platforms, the system localizes audible events (e.g., gas leaks) and inaudible robot commands with ≥95% accuracy under 86 dB factory noise while sustaining 9.32 ms/° real-time processing. The communication layer achieves 0% word-error rate to 10 m and up to 85 dB noise with 0.24 ms/word on-device decoding. Building on role-specialized agents (Planning + Execution), we demonstrate 100% success across 21 one-way humanoid→quadruped commands and scalable hierarchical teamwork (humanoid commander + vision-expert Leader + sound-expert Follower) completing six representative inspection missions with ~38–39 s cycle times. Together, these results show that sound can serve as a practical backbone for resilient, interpretable, and infrastructure-independent multi-robot collaboration.
